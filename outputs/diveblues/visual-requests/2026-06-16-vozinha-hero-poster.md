```text
Create a vertical 4:5 Instagram poster reference image, 1080 x 1350 composition. Theme: a World Cup cultural hero poster about an unnamed 40-year-old Black goalkeeper from a small underdog nation, inspired by American ordinary-hero sports storytelling. Do not depict any real player exactly. Do not use the name Vozinha. Do not use official World Cup, FIFA, federation, club, or national team logos. The image should feel like a serious sports documentary poster mixed with subtle graphic-novel heroism.

Main subject: an older Black goalkeeper, around 40 years old, wearing a plain unbranded yellow/gold goalkeeper top and worn goalkeeper gloves. He stands alone near the goal line after the final whistle, head lowered, shoulders trembling slightly, sweat and tears visible, hands hanging down with gloves still on. He looks exhausted but unbroken. Behind him: an abstract football goal, dark blue night stadium, harsh white floodlights, blurred crowd silhouettes, and faint motion trails of multiple footballs like the shots he stopped. Add subtle green, red, blue, and white abstract light accents inspired by Cabo Verde colors, but do not show an official flag or crest.

Mood: quiet heroism, late recognition, ordinary man who survived an impossible night. Not a celebration poster, not a product ad. The emotion is restrained, heavy, human, and cinematic. Style: American sports documentary poster, gritty paper texture, slight comic-book ink shading, strong contrast, film grain, dramatic rim light. Composition: the goalkeeper occupies the lower-middle 50% of the image; leave clean headline space at the top for “THE KEEPER WHO STAYED.” and a smaller subtitle near the bottom for “A draw that felt like a life.” No product, no brand logo, no official kit, no real match photography, no trophy, no player numbers, no copyrighted sports branding.
```
