1. “A Diamond Is Forever” – De
   Beers (1947)

-   Impact: Literally created the
    expectation that diamond rings =
    engagement.

-   Legacy: Forever altered marriage
    customs, entrenched as a global
    social norm.

-   Scale: Few brand ideas have directly
    changed personal life events as
    much as this.
2. “Just Do It” – Nike (1988)

- Impact: Transformed the brand into
  an ideology of perseverance and
  personal achievement.

- Legacy: Still relevant decades later,
  shaping self-belief narratives in
  sports and beyond.

- Scale: Went beyond advertising to
  influence attitudes on performance
  and human potential.
3. “I’d Like to Buy the World a
Coke” – Coca-Cola (1971)
- Impact: Captured and fuelled the 1970s
  countercultural desire for unity and
  harmony.

- Legacy: Cemented Coke’s emotional role
  as a unifier, though its direct impact faded
  over time.

- Scale: Defined advertising’s ability to
  reflect and shape global optimism.
