Cultural Innovation:
Success comes from innovating better ideologies (ideas) rather than better
features.


Breaking Orthodoxy:
Most brands adopt similar cultural expressions (orthodoxy), innovators identify
and break from these conventions to address emerging ideological needs.



Opportunity through Disruption:
Social change create moments when established cultural expressions lose
relevance, opening opportunities for brands with fresh ideological approaches.


Multiple Value Sources
Cultural innovations generate symbolic value (meaning), social value (identity/status),
and a functional halo effect (perceived quality improvements).

"Aspirational Adventurers"

known for their exciting and varied lifestyles.

performance. They make quick decisions,

spirits and busy lives.

just a way to get around; it's a key part of who
they are and what they do every day.

The HiLux is built to a different standard. Its legendary reliability
  is an unbroken truth that endures from one generation to the
 next. This truth is not merely passed down as advice but as an
                     instinctive understanding.

 However, this instinct has been overshadowed by the allure of
sleek aesthetics and the promise of ever-increasing capabilities
                        from competitors.

          It's time to reawaken that instinctive choice.

 By highlighting the times when this instinct comes to the fore,
 and people naturally turn to the HiLux to get the job done, we
   can bring this deep-seated truth back to the forefront of
                         people's minds.
