1. Sensitivity to Problems and Gaps
James Watson and Francis Crick, knew genetic material existed but faced a
significant gap in knowledge about its molecular structure and how genetic
information was stored and transmitted.

2. Searching for Solutions
Watson and Crick actively searched for answers by studying existing research,
including crucial X-ray diffraction images from Rosalind Franklin, and identified
what was missing.

3. Formulating Hypotheses
They formed hypotheses, built physical models, tested various structures, and
learned from each failure.

4. Testing and Retesting
Watson and Crick repeatedly built physical models of DNA, testing various
chemical configurations and rejecting earlier incorrect models, such as the triple-
helix structure, after rigorous scrutiny against experimental data.

5. Communicating the Results
They communicated their groundbreaking discovery clearly in the journal
Nature (1953), changing science forever.
