HOW TO UNLOCK A
CONSUMER INSIGHT

1. Problem behaviour analysis:
Identifying patterns in
consumer behaviour

2. Consumer narrative
Unveiling their logic behind choices

3. Emotional mapping:
Examining the feelings and
emotions around the
experience

4. Motivational insights:
Deciphering the causes
behind emotional responses

5. Current resolutions:
Analysing behavioural
responses to the problem
UNLOCKING A CONSUMER INSIGHT
QUITTING SMOKING


1. Problem behaviour analysis:
Consumers repeatedly attempt to quit smoking, averaging 7.2 tries before seeking
external assistance.

2. Consumer narrative.
Deep-seated frustration with their inability to control their smoking habit.

3. Emotional mapping:
They often don’t feel like themselves and capitulate to these negative emotions

4. Motivational insights:
They hate the loss of control over who they are (self-identity)

5. Current resolutions:
In response to these feelings of loss of control and frustration, many smokers revert to
smoking as a means to regain a sense of control.

Sophie finally kicked off her shoes after a long day at the office, sinking into her couch. She'd
been dreaming about this moment—just relaxing, ordering takeout, and enjoying a quiet
evening watching something good. She grabbed the remote and began scrolling through her
streaming apps. At first, she felt excitement, marveling at all the options available. Comedy
specials, crime documentaries, trendy new series her coworkers wouldn’t stop talking about—
so many tempting choices.
But ten minutes turned to thirty, and Sophie's enthusiasm began to fade. Each thumbnail
suddenly looked the same, and every choice seemed uncertain. She felt a wave of anxiety creep
in, the relaxing evening slipping away as indecision took hold. She thought to herself, "What if I
pick the wrong show and waste my entire night?"
Sophie recalled how last weekend she tried a new drama everyone raved about, only to feel
bored twenty minutes in. She hated the idea of repeating that disappointment tonight,
especially after such an exhausting day. Glancing at her phone, she noticed several unread
messages from friends, another reminder of how limited her precious downtime was. The
thought made her more stressed rather than relaxed.
Finally, almost reflexively, Sophie clicked on an old sitcom she'd watched dozens of times. It
wasn't new or exciting, but at least she knew it wouldn't let her down. As the familiar opening
credits began, she sighed with relief, her tension easing slightly. It wasn't what she'd initially
envisioned, but it was safe, comforting. Tomorrow, maybe, she'd try something new again—or
maybe she wouldn't.
