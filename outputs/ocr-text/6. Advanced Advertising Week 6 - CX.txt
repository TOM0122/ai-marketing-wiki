Customer Journey Mapping
What’s a Journey
Map?
• A visual representation of a customer’s
  experience.

• A tool to help us understand how customers
  interact with a brand in context of the world
  around them.

• An outline of what customers are doing,
  thinking and feeling, and the various
  touchpoints they interact with.

• A method of building empathy for the
  customer by identifying pain points.

• A collection of opportunities to improve
  each step of the customer’s experience.

How to create a customer journey map
1. Setting Objectives


Define what you want to achieve with the
customer journey map. Are you looking to
improve customer satisfaction, increase
conversion, or identify pain points in the
current journey?


Purpose:
Clear objectives guide the scope and focus
of the mapping process, ensuring that all
efforts align with your business goals.




                                             24
2. Gathering Data


Collect data from various sources to
understand how customers interact with
your brand. This includes analytics from your
website, customer feedback, CRM data, social
media interactions, and input from customer-
facing employees.


Purpose:
Comprehensive data collection provides a
factual basis for mapping the real customer
journey, highlighting actual behavior and
experiences.
3. Creating Buyer Personas

Develop detailed personas for your typical
customers. Each persona should include
demographic information, behaviours,
preferences, goals, and pain points.

Purpose:
Personas help personalize the journey
map, making it easier to understand
diverse customer needs and how different
segments interact with your brand.
4. Mapping the Journey


Start plotting the journey for each
persona, from initial awareness to post
purchase interactions. Include all
touchpoints, emotions, pain points, and
opportunities.


Purpose:
This visual representation helps identify
key interactions, areas for improvement,
and moments of truth that significantly
impact the customer experience.
5. Analyzing and Identifying Opportunities

Analyze the journey map to identify gaps
between the current customer experience
and the ideal state. Look for patterns in pain
points and high points, and identify
opportunities to enhance the journey.

Purpose:
This stage focuses on understanding where
you can make practical changes or
improvements to impact customer
satisfaction and business outcomes
positively.
6. Optimizing and Iterating

Implement the changes identified in the
previous stage and monitor how they
affect the customer experience. Use
customer feedback and updated data to
continually refine and optimize the
journey.

Purpose:
Continuous improvement ensures that the
customer journey keeps pace with
changing customer expectations and
business goals.

PAIN POINT:
Taking your laptop out of your bag at
security checking.

It's time-consuming and cumbersome
to unpack and repack laptops and
liquids at busy airport security
checkpoints.

SOLUTION
Melbourne Airport, in partnership with
Virgin Australia, has piloted advanced
security technology that allows
travelers to leave laptops and liquids in
their bags during security screenings.
PAIN POINT:
Uncertainty in furniture shopping.

Customers often struggle to visualise
how furniture will look and fit in their
homes, which can lead to
dissatisfaction and returns.

SOLUTION
IKEA developed the Place app, which
uses augmented reality (AR) to let
customers visualize how furniture and
products will look and fit in their
homes before buying.
OPPORTUNITY:
Continuous improvement of vehicle
performance post-purchase.

Traditionally, cars cannot be upgraded or
improved without mechanical
modifications or recalls.

SOLUTION:
Tesla's over-the-air software updates
significantly enhance the customer
experience by improving the car's
performance, safety features, and even
adding new functionalities after the
purchase.
PAIN POINT:
The risk associated with buying
eyewear online.

Customers are often unsure about how
glasses will look and fit, which can
discourage online purchases.

SOLUTION:
Warby Parker offers a Home Try-On
program where customers can select
five pairs of glasses to be shipped to
their home for free.
OPPORTUNITY:
Personalised engagement with music
streaming users.

Why it's needed: Users of music
streaming services often lack
personalised interaction, which can
affect user engagement and
subscription loyalty.

SOLUTION:
Each year, Spotify provides its users
with Wrapped, a personalised
summary of their most listened to
songs, genres, and artists over the past
year.

utilize the full capabilities of their devices
due to a lack of knowledge, leading to
missed opportunities for deeper product
appreciation and ecosystem buy-in.

Challenge: Identify engaging
approaches Apple could use to educate
and inspire users post-purchase,
unlocking product potential, and
reinforcing ecosystem loyalty.
Path-to-Purchase: Sephora
Beauty Products


Scenario: Shoppers struggle to
confidently select beauty products
online due to uncertainty around
suitability (shade, skin type), often
leading to abandoned carts or
unnecessary returns.

Challenge: Devise solutions Sephora
could offer to build shopper confidence,
increase accuracy in product selection,
and create a frictionless online
shopping experience.
Path-to-Purchase: IKEA In-
store Experience

Scenario: Customers visiting IKEA stores
become frustrated by complex store
layouts, difficulty locating items quickly, and
long checkout lines, negatively impacting
their overall experience.

Challenge: Propose solutions IKEA could
implement to streamline in-store
navigation, enhance convenience, and
improve the shopping journey, turning
frustration into satisfaction.
