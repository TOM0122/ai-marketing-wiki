CONNECTIONS
PLANNING
A marketing strategy that focuses on building
meaningful relationships between brands and
consumers by understanding the customer
journey and tailoring communication to specific
touchpoints and moments.
    Key
Principles of
Connections
 Planning:
Spotify Wrapped:
Hyper-Personalisation as Social Currency

Strategy:
Spotify turned personalised listening data into
sharable content, tapping into people’s desire to
share their identity through music.

Creative Execution:
Spotify Wrapped provides a personalised
summary of a user’s year in music. Leveraging
customisable, social-ready visuals, it transforms
personal data into relatable, share-worthy social
currency.

Why it Worked:
• Capitalised on the cultural trend of identity
  expression.
• Made the audience active participants, not
  passive consumers.
• Drove organic brand advocacy by turning
  users into promoters.
Snickers Hungerithm:
Real-Time Emotional Targeting

Strategy:
Targeted consumers based on mood fluctuations
expressed on social media, turning real-time
social sentiment into dynamic promotional
pricing.

Creative Execution:
The Snickers Hungerithm monitored Twitter
sentiment, dropping Snickers’ prices in 7-Eleven
stores as the online mood worsened—connecting
digital conversations directly to retail purchases.

Why it Worked:
•Brilliant use of real-time consumer context
(negative moods) to motivate purchase.
•Cleverly tied online conversation directly to offline
actions.
•Dramatically boosted sales and social buzz.
Burger King Whopper Detour:
Geoconquesting & Mobile Integration

Strategy:
Burger King leveraged location-based technology
to directly engage its competitor's customers
(McDonald's) and convert them into BK
customers.

Creative Execution:
The campaign used mobile geofencing, offering a
one-cent Whopper—but only if users ordered it
through the Burger King app while physically
near a McDonald's location.

Why it Worked:
• Playful, provocative, and immediately
  engaging.
• Merged digital/mobile context with real-world
  interaction.
• Skyrocketed app downloads, store visits, and
  consumer buzz.
Pedigree SelfieSTIX:
Social Moments & Product Integration

Strategy:
Pedigree identified a novel moment—the challenge of
capturing selfies with pets—and created an innovative
product tie-in.

Creative Execution:
A smartphone attachment called "SelfieSTIX" held a
Pedigree treat to get dogs’ attention, ensuring perfect
selfies with pets. Supported by a playful social campaign,
users shared their pet selfies, organically promoting the
brand.

Why it Worked:
•Created new, highly shareable consumer behaviour.
•Seamlessly integrated brand and product into social
moments.
•Amplified through user-generated content and social
advocacy.

Connections planning is a
strategic framework that    Typical Process of Connections Planning:
guides how the              1. Define Objectives
campaign will be rolled          Clarify marketing and business objectives clearly, e.g.,
                                 increase brand awareness, consideration, sales, or
out to achieve its               loyalty.
                            2. Audience Segmentation and Insight
objectives.                      Identify and understand target segments deeply
                                 (behavioural, psychographic, cultural insights).
                            3. Consumer Journey Mapping
                                 Map the consumer journey to identify critical
                                 moments, needs, motivations, and barriers.
                            4. Connection Points Analysis
                                 Identify the most impactful touchpoints (moments,
                                 channels, contexts) across consumer journeys.
                            5. Engagement Strategy Development
                                 Create a strategic framework that outlines which
                                 channels and contexts will be leveraged, how
                                 messages will be sequenced, and what behaviours
                                 each interaction aims to drive.
                            6. Channel Planning & Integration
                                 Determine the optimal mix of paid, owned, earned, and
                                 shared media channels.
                            7. Measurement & Iteration
                                 Establish clear KPIs, track effectiveness, and adjust
                                 based on ongoing insights and results.

  Finding Their Edge in a

Koala Mattress
(Australia)

Who they are:
An Australian brand known
for shaking up the traditional
mattress industry with its
direct-to-consumer, "bed-in-
a-box" model

Brand Vibe:
Think innovative, proudly
Australian.
THE CHALLENGE

Koala made waves when it launched,
offering a simpler way to buy a mattress
with quick delivery and a "no worries"
trial period. This model was disruptive.
However, the mattress market, especially
online, has become incredibly crowded.

Many competitors now offer similar
products and conveniences. In this busy
space, Koala needs to ensure its unique
voice and value proposition are clearly
heard and resonate with consumers.
Key Issues
The task
In a market full of similar
options, how can Koala
sharpen its distinctive edge
and strengthen its
connection with
consumers? Your task is to
develop an advertising
campaign that helps Koala
stand out and become a
preferred choice again.
Campaign Goals:
Your campaign should aim to:

Who We're Talking To:

Main Audience:
Primarily Australians aged 25-55
(Millennials & Gen X). They are
comfortable shopping online, look
for good value, care about the
environment, and often appreciate
authentic Australian brands.

Also Consider:
People buying their first home, city
renters, or individuals going
through life changes (moving, new
relationships etc.) who might need
a new mattress.
Key Areas to Explore (Strategic Thinking):
As you develop your strategy, consider these
areas:

1.   Australian Roots: How can Koala
     authentically use its Australian heritage as
     a meaningful point of difference?

2. Sustainability Story: What's Koala's genuine
   commitment to sustainability, and how
   can this be communicated convincingly?

3. Product Distinction: Beyond the
   convenience, what truly sets Koala's
   products apart from the competition?
   (Think features, design, materials etc.)

4. Emotional Resonance: What feeling or
   emotional space can Koala uniquely own in
   the minds of consumers, beyond just being
   a mattress?
