Brands Grow by Winning
More Occasional Buyers, Not
by Changing Buying Habits.

Most of a brand’s buyers only
purchase once or twice a year
— whether the brand is
growing, declining, or stable.
Brands mainly
compete as “mere
lookalikes”, with
consumers not really
distinguishing one
from another, thus
brand loyalty doesn’t
really exist.

Customers Aren’t

Double Jeopardy: Why Small
Brands Struggle Twice
Smaller brands face a double
disadvantage: fewer customers and
weaker loyalty. When market share falls,
they lose customers faster than loyalty,
meaning penetration declines more
quickly than purchase frequency does.
