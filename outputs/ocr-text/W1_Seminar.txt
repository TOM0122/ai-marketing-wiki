What is Marketing Research?

Marketing research is the function that links the
consumer, customer, and public to the marketer
through information—information used to identify
and define opportunities and problems; generate,
refine, and evaluate actions; monitor performance;
and improve understanding of it as a process. It
specifies the information required to address these
issues, designs the method for collecting
information, manages and implements the data
collection process, analyzes the results, and
communicates the findings and their implications.


(American Marketing Association 2017).                5

How to become a marketing researcher?

 1. Consider studying a Bachelor of Business, Management, Economics or Commerce
    with a major in marketing. These courses will take you 3 years to complete full-time.
 2. Alternatively, consider a Bachelor of Statistics, Psychology, Sociology, Economics,
    Behavioural Sciences or Computer Science degrees. These are less common
    pathways, but may help you to specialise in particular industries.
 3. Consider joining the Australian Market & Social Research Society to keep up with
    industry trends and developments.

What do market researchers do? (Seek.com)

 1. Meeting with clients to agree project scope and timeline.
 2. Writing and managing the distribution of surveys.
 3. Briefing research teams and managing survey staff.
 4. Managing project budgets.
 5. Conducting qualitative, quantitative and ethnographic research, such as surveys,
    interviews, focus groups and observations.
 6. Using statistical modelling software.
 7. Organising, managing and storing data and information.
 8. Analysing data and interpreting patterns.
 9. Writing reports and presenting data-driven results.
 10. Consulting with clients on how to best utilise the findings.

First Step: Identify and Formulate the
Problem (the most important step!)
     1. What? = Marketing Symptom
      The marketing symptom can be a problem (decreasing sales figures,
      declining customer satisfaction, etc.), as well as an opportunity
      (emerging markets opportunities, access to new technology, etc.)
     2. Why? = Marketing Problem
      1. Ambiguous problems
         (Uncertainty regarding the measures and leverages of problem
         solution)
      2. Somewhat defined problems
         (Uncertainty regarding the interaction of the measures)
      3. Clearly defined problems
         (Interaction of the measures and leverages is known, but the
         dimension is not clear)

• Some questions are not politically acceptable

• Some decisions have already been taken
