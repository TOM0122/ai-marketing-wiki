          Recap: Tests for Means
Compare a single mean to a test value (pre-specified by researcher)
• H0: μ = test value    univariate
• One sample t-test
Compare two means
• H0: μ1 = μ2     bivariate
• If μ1 and μ2 are from the same population: paired samples t-test
• If μ1 and μ2 are from two different populations: independent samples t-test
Compare more than two population means (m groups)
• H0: μ1 = μ2 = μ3 = … = μm     multivariate
• One-way ANOVA

            Important Notes about (Pearson) Correlation
            Coefficient: r
•   r describes a ”linear relationship” between two variables
    • r = 0: NO linear relationship
       • However, there could still be a curvilinear relationship:

    •   r = ±1: a straight line can describe the relationship

•   Coefficient of determination (r2) = r*r
    • % of the variation in one variable can be accounted by the other one
    • E.g., r2 = 0.36 (r = 0.6) means that 36% of the variation in one variable can be
      explained by the other variable
    • Similar idea as “R-square” in a bivariate regression model (with one IV; one DV)

           Important Notes about Correlation
           Coefficient: r
• Correlation does NOT indicate causality
 • E.g. Increase in Christmas card sales does not cause Christmas;
• Statistical significance is NOT the same as substantive significance (or economic significance,
  practical significance).
 • E.g. Unit sales = 100 + 0.0005*Ad Spending in $
 – Every $10,000 spending will lead to 5 more units sales
 – If the unit price is low, then little substantive significance
• Correlation is NOT interaction
 • Interaction: TWO independent variables act together to affect your dependent variable. E.g.,
   the attitude (Y) toward the advertisements (X1) varies with the mood (X2) and the
   interaction (X1*X2)

            Chi-Square Analysis
❑ Examine whether two nominal variables are associated in the population
• Summarize in a two-way frequency table, “cross-tabulation (crosstab)”
• The test compares the observed frequencies of the responses with the expected frequencies
• Does not assess direction of the relationship
• Examples: the relationship between the following variables, including socio-demographic(e.g., sex,
  education groups, income groups), Ad exposure (Y/N), Purchase (Y/N), Brand choice (Coke/Pepsi/Dr.
  Pepper)
Assumptions for Chi-Square test
• Independence of observation
  − Each observation should be generated by a different subject and no subject is counted twice
• Size of expected frequencies
  – Ideally the minimum expected frequency for each variable/cell should be 5

Example
In this test, we hypothesized that there is an association between the home country and the traveler's
status. The result of a Chi-Square test shows a significant association between these two variables
(𝑥 2 (6, 1065) = 35.638, p < .001). It was found that among USA travelers, 77.9% have blue status, and

            Regression Analysis
Importance of regression analysis
• Regression analysis is one of the most important methods in quantitative market research
• Marketing and strategy departments of major companies (e.g. Procter & Gamble, BMW Group, Nestlé)
  use regression analysis regularly for decision-making


Regression analysis can
    1. Indicate if independent variables have a significant relationship with a dependent variable.
    2. Indicate the relative strength of different independent variables’ effects on a dependent variable.
    3. Make predictions.



* In this lecture we introduce linear regression (OLS)

The Regression Analysis Estimate a Line Using
the Observations

                      The regression estimation procedure
                      attempts to fit a best possible line. This line
                      is optimal if the squared distances to all
                      observation points are minimized. Thereby:
                      1.   Positive and negative deviances don’t
                           balance each other
                      2.   Bigger deviances are weighted
                           disproportionately high
                      If the assumptions of the regression are
                      harmed, the line might not be the true line!
