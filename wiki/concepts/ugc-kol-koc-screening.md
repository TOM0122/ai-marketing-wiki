# UGC, KOL, KOC Screening

## Source

- `raw/files/IG红人建联SOP交接文档.docx`

## Definition

Screening is the first judgment step before replying to inbound Instagram creators.

The source SOP focuses on creators who already DM the brand, not cold outbound discovery.

## Screening Inputs

Check before first reply:

- follower count
- engagement
- recent content performance
- content style
- product category fit
- brand tone fit

## Initial Classification

Classify each inbound creator into one of three working buckets:

1. Free product exchange / UGC candidate
2. Paid collaboration candidate
3. Not worth pursuing

## Free Product Exchange Candidate

Good enough when:

- content style is acceptable
- product-category fit is reasonable
- creator is willing to post for product
- content can help accumulate UGC

Do not over-judge production quality here.

The main control point is whether they actually post after receiving product.

## Paid Collaboration Candidate

Needs stricter review.

Check:

- whether audience and style fit the brand
- whether recent content looks real and useful
- whether engagement supports paid spend
- whether quote can be negotiated to target range
- whether a clear script or topic direction can be agreed

Paid candidates should be confirmed with the owner after price and suitability look reasonable.

## Red Flags

- quote far beyond budget
- style mismatch with product or brand
- weak engagement relative to follower count
- unclear content direction
- slow or evasive communication after product shipment
- no output near the expected cycle

## Tracking Logic

Screening is incomplete unless the collaboration is later tracked.

The tracker should make visible:

- who received product
- who was paid
- who has posted
- who needs follow-up
- who may have disappeared
