## Strategic Premise

Ocoopa social content should not start from product specs.

It should start from moments where users experience cold, discomfort, or body-state disruption but do not usually verbalize it.

## Core Content Claim

```text
You never said it was cold. But your hands remember.
```

## Content Pillars

### 1. Problem-Driven Warmth

Own unexpected, unprepared cold.

Use scenes where the user did not plan for cold but feels it immediately:

- red-eye flight
- airport lounge
- early car start
- waiting outside for five minutes
- over-air-conditioned office
- train window seat

Content job:

- create recognition
- make the discomfort visible
- position Ocoopa as first response

### 2. Value Reframing

Reframe the product from "heat tool" to "grip-based comfort object".

Sub-angles:

- tactile grounding
- soft wellness
- emotional regulation
- EDC object
- light body-care routine

Content job:

- upgrade category perception
- connect function with feeling
- make the product feel carried, not only used

### 3. Audience-Based Desirability

Build different entry points for different users.

Audience modules:

- EDC / gear users
- travel and outdoor users
- lifestyle and gifting users
- wellness and body-state users

Content job:

- let each group find one believable reason to want the product
- avoid one generic "winter product" message

## Content Path

```text
Hook -> Story -> Value
```

### Hook

Start with a felt contrast.

Examples:

- "you thought it would not be cold"
- "office AC is not cold, it is brutal"
- "waiting five minutes was enough"
- "can it heat this?"
- "what if warmth was something you could hold?"

### Story

Show a small situation changing.

The structure:

```text
unexpected cold -> discomfort -> product trial -> relief / control -> comment prompt
```

### Value

Do not stop at "warm".

Value types:

- functional relief
- emotional recognition
- grounding / regulation
- lifestyle fit
- community participation

## Practical Rule

Ocoopa should not sound like it is selling a gadget.

It should sound like it noticed a small discomfort that users usually ignore.

## Campaign PDF Addition

The brand reshaping PDF gives social content a clearer campaign role:

```text
social is the window for brand personality, emotion, and attitude
```

It should also act as the topic and traffic engine, especially by strengthening:

- fun
- topicality
- tacit everyday scenes
- relaxed real-life states
- content that feels close to consumer life

The PDF also adds a production rule:

```text
erase camera-consciousness as much as possible
```

For Ocoopa social, that means less "model demonstrates product" and more "a real person reaches for warmth inside a believable moment".

Social should coordinate with Amazon, DTC, EDM, packaging, retail, and B2B materials through one shared narrative logic:

```text
product + copy + visual
```

If those three layers drift apart, the consumer has to work harder to understand the brand, and the campaign loses force.
